Be warned, this is a bit of a rant.

I'll admit, CakePHP work is interesting to me. I use it as a learning tool and constantly tinker with various extensions. For example, I have a project to auto-generate an admin section based on the db config, and one for documentation in the form of tutorials. I run a CakePHP plugin index solely so student developers at my university wouldn't have to look in 95 places for existing code.

Obstacles

That said, I have a job at a wonderful startup that constantly challenges my ability to not fuck up - it's easier said than done! I'm currently finishing a BS in CS with a minor in Mathematics - and maybe Philosophy if I can get my act together - which means that finding a job where I would be a good fit is difficult. Here are the main obstacles:

I am in downtown manhattan. Not Miami. Not Michigan. Not Chicago.

What do these mean? Well, if you are in Miami or anywhere else in the world, I will only do remote work. I will NOT be on-site. This means that your job offer must be a remote if not in NYC.

My schedule is difficult because of classes. Standups do not happen for me without holiday

I have classes to attend. I will not be on call. I will attempt to work around this when possible, but if you require me to be on a conference call, or a standup, I will be mostly unable to comply. Class will come first.

I like working at night

I work at night all the time. Does your company need a night owl? If so, great, we'll probably get along awesomely. Can you handle payroll for night hours? If not, I probably won't be able to work with you. See the above paragraph for more info on why.

I am likely younger than your entire team

Please treat me as any other developer, not as a kid. If I mess up, let me know, and if I am doing great, good. This is important if only because I look a lot younger than I am.

I do a lot of open-source CakePHP work. I will not stop doing so.

I work on a lot of CakePHP open source projects. If taking a job at your company conflicts with this, it was nice talking to you. My current work is all non-CakePHP, so I don't think I have much of an issue. I will not compete with your application/client work. I may, at some future time, reuse the knowledge I've gained to create something useful. That is at my discretion, but do know that I will ensure that your IP remains your IP.

I work for a startup already

Since I already work for a startup, I can't do anything in the ticketing industry for some number of years. It's in my contract. If you require more than freelance, I will require above and beyond what they provide in flexibility and mental stimulation.

I take slight offense when people make fun of my current job in some way. My coworkers and the cofounders are great and we've built an awesome application. I've learned a lot here, and have spent many nights toiling on complex features. Hopefully I can say the same about your company :)
